

# Customer Health

![Power BI Customer Health Dashboard](../assets/Customer_Health.png)

## Overview

The Customer Health dashboard provides a comprehensive view of customer value distribution and engagement status across the customer base. It helps identify where revenue is generated, how customer value is distributed geographically, and which customer segments require attention.

The dashboard combines customer lifetime value segmentation with customer health stages to evaluate revenue concentration and potential retention risks.

## Business Questions

This dashboard helps answer:

- Which customer segments generate the highest revenue?
- How is customer value distributed across cities?
- What percentage of customers are actively engaged?
- Which customer groups are showing signs of churn risk?
- Where should retention efforts be prioritized?

## Key Metrics

| Metric | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Total Customers | Total customer count in the dataset |
| Average Customer Lifetime Revenue | Average revenue generated by a customer during their lifetime |
| Average Customer Order Value | Average revenue per transaction |

## Customer Value Distribution Across Cities

The geographic map visualizes customer value concentration across Türkiye.

### Insights

- Revenue is heavily concentrated in major metropolitan areas.
- İstanbul contributes the largest share of total revenue.
- The top seven cities generate approximately **43% of total revenue**, indicating strong geographic concentration.
- Regional expansion opportunities may exist in lower-performing cities.

### Business Impact

Understanding geographic concentration allows targeted regional marketing campaigns and localized growth strategies.

## Revenue Distribution by Customer Value Segment

Customers are grouped into four value segments:

- Low Value
- Medium Value
- High Value
- VIP

### Insights

- Medium Value customers generate the largest share of revenue.
- High Value customers represent a smaller population but contribute significantly to overall revenue.
- VIP customers contribute a relatively small share of total revenue, suggesting an opportunity to expand this segment.

### Business Impact

Retention and upsell initiatives targeting Medium Value customers can generate the highest short-term revenue impact.

## Customer Health Distribution by Value Segment

Customers are classified into three health stages:

| Stage | Meaning |
|---------|----------|
| Active | Regularly purchasing customers |
| Warm | Customers showing reduced activity |
| At Risk | Customers with elevated churn risk |

### Insights

- Higher-value customers tend to have better health scores.
- VIP customers show the highest Active rate.
- Low-value customers exhibit the highest churn risk.
- Medium Value customers account for the largest revenue contribution and therefore require close monitoring.

### Business Impact

Customer health monitoring enables proactive retention campaigns before revenue is lost.

## Recommended Actions

### Short-Term

- Launch retention campaigns targeting At Risk customers.
- Focus engagement initiatives on Medium Value customers.
- Monitor declining activity in Warm segments.

### Long-Term

- Increase the share of High Value and VIP customers.
- Develop regional growth strategies outside the top revenue-generating cities.
- Implement predictive churn monitoring models.

# Customer Retention & RFM Analysis

![Power BI Customer Health Dashboard](../assets/Customer_Retention_RFM_Analysis.png)

## Overview

The Customer Retention & RFM Analysis dashboard evaluates customer loyalty and retention using RFM segmentation principles.

RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) analysis categorizes customers based on their purchasing behavior and identifies customers who are most likely to generate future revenue or churn.

## Business Questions

This dashboard helps answer:

- Which customer groups contribute the most revenue?
- How much revenue is currently at risk?
- Which customers should be prioritized for retention?
- How does revenue distribution differ across RFM segments?
- What is the relationship between customer recency and value?

## Key Metrics

| Metric | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| At Risk Customers | Customers showing signs of disengagement |
| Active Customer Rate | Percentage of currently active customers |
| Revenue At Risk | Revenue associated with customers likely to churn |

## Revenue Concentration by Customer Recency

This visualization explores the relationship between customer value (CLV) and recency.

### Insights

- Customer value generally decreases as recency increases.
- Recent customers contribute disproportionately higher lifetime value.
- Champions and Loyal Customers exhibit the strongest revenue concentration.

### Business Impact

Recent customer engagement is a strong indicator of future revenue generation.

## RFM Segment Composition

Customers are divided into four key segments:

| Segment | Description |
|-----------|------------|
| Champions | Highly engaged, high-value customers |
| Loyal Customers | Consistently purchasing customers |
| Potential Loyalists | Customers likely to become loyal |
| At Risk | Customers with declining engagement |

### Insights

- Potential Loyalists represent the largest revenue share.
- Loyal Customers form a significant portion of recurring revenue.
- At Risk customers account for a substantial revenue base that may be lost without intervention.

### Business Impact

Moving Potential Loyalists into Loyal or Champion status represents a major growth opportunity.

## Revenue At Risk by Customer Value Segment

This chart highlights the amount of revenue potentially lost if At Risk customers churn.

### Insights

- Medium Value customers account for the largest portion of revenue at risk.
- Revenue risk is concentrated in segments with the largest customer populations.
- VIP customers represent minimal immediate revenue risk.

### Business Impact

Retention efforts targeting Medium Value customers offer the highest potential return on investment.

## Revenue Share by RFM Segment and Gender

This analysis compares revenue contribution across genders within each RFM segment.

### Insights

- Revenue distribution is relatively balanced across genders.
- No significant gender-specific revenue dependency is observed.
- Retention strategies can focus primarily on behavioral segmentation rather than demographic segmentation.

### Business Impact

Customer behavior is a stronger predictor of revenue than demographic attributes in this dataset.

## Recommended Actions

### Immediate Priorities

1. Re-engage At Risk customers through targeted campaigns.
2. Protect Medium Value customers from churn.
3. Convert Potential Loyalists into Loyal Customers.

### Strategic Priorities

1. Increase Champion customer share.
2. Implement predictive churn modeling.
3. Develop personalized loyalty programs.
4. Monitor customer health metrics continuously.
